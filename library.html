Outreach in the Library

2025  ·  by Team 23928

Last year, Double Quarter Pounders (FTC 23928) headed to the local library to share our passion for engineering with the community. We set up a demo station in the meeting room, turning a quiet Saturday afternoon into a hands-on exploration of how our competition robot actually works.

The highlight for many of the visitors was the chance to take the controls. We let students of all ages grab the gamepads to test-drive the bot, while we used the room's displays to walk parents through our design and coding process. It was a great opportunity to move beyond the competition field and show the "how" and "why" behind every gear and line of code.

These library visits are a core part of our mission to make STEM feel accessible. There's a special kind of energy that happens when a child realizes they can actually command a machine they've only ever seen in videos. Whether we were troubleshooting a turn with a toddler or discussing sensors with a middle schooler, the goal was to prove that robotics is for everyone.

We're incredibly grateful to the library staff for hosting us and to all the families who brought such great questions and curiosity. Building these local connections reminds our team why we do this, and we're already looking forward to our next community session.
